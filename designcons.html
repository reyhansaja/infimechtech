The Needs of CFD Engineers in the Industry 4.0 Era


As we enter the era of Industry 4.0, the demand for CFD engineers is on the rise, accompanied by the influx of digital transformation across various sectors, particularly in the industrial sector. With digital computing models, engineers are expected to keep pace with the times by transforming conventional methods into digital models, one of which is through Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD). It is hoped that with proficiency in CFD, engineers can accelerate production lines in the industry and reduce the costs associated with design, optimization, and product analysis by using CFD simulations.

Where CFD serves as a tool for analyzing fluid flow, heat transfer, and associated transport phenomena such as chemical or electrical reactions based on computer simulations. Although initially developed by the aerospace industry, CFD has now become a crucial tool in various other industries such as automotive, power generation, chemical, nuclear, maritime, and more. Over the past decade, several non-mechanical industries like electronics, medical, and environmental have even adopted CFD for research and development (R&D) purposes.

Therefore, CFD is suitable for industries implementing rapid processing, rapid prototyping, and design firms to optimize product designs before production. Based on this background, we, as the Infimech team, would like to participate in organizing Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) training at your institution.

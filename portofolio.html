Air Circulation Analysis at Lippo Mall Condenser

The adjacent image represents the numerical analysis results of the outdoor condenser space ventilation simulation on the rooftop of a public building. The image displays color projections corresponding to the spectrum below. It can be observed that the highest air velocity is located at the condenser fan and duct, with some air exiting towards the open space.

The image shows that the air velocity from the outdoor system exhibits a pattern similar to each fan layer, and the air velocity is not evenly distributed, but rather concentrated around the fan area. This makes it susceptible to backflow in the condenser air stream.

Client : PT. Jaya Teknik Indonesia.
Analysis of Loading on Centrifugal Pump Shaft

With Finite Element Analysis (FEA), engineers can understand the loading pattern on the shaft of a centrifugal pump. Through this simulation, it is determined how the blade load affects the strain and deflection values of the shaft.

The image on the side displays the deformation distribution, with red indicating the highest deformation and blue indicating the smallest. The FEA simulation depicted here utilizes Steel S185 as the material.

Client : PT. Torishima Guna Engineering
Oscillating Water Coloum Power Generation

The contour on the side is an image of the Oscillating Water Column (OWC) Power Generator, which serves as an air compressor. In the image, you can see the pressure distribution spectrum of the turbine. The darker areas indicate a significant pressure drop due to the air passing through the Guide Vane towards the turbine blades.

For this case study, we are examining the extent of the pressure drop that occurs across the Guide Vane and Turbine Blade, as well as the amount of power that can be obtained from a compressor pressure of 144 PSI.

Client : PT. Medco Energy International, Tbk.
Thermal Storage Tank

To understand how fluid flow rate can maintain temperature stability in a thermal water storage tank, one can perform Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) simulations. In these simulations, the user is conducting several runs with varying flow rate values to determine the time it takes for the temperature to reach the specified limit.

It can be observed in the image that the high-temperature intake is represented by the red-colored region, and the water with lower temperature, which absorbs heat from the incoming water, undergoes a color change from greenish-blue to red. From this simulation, the time it takes for the water temperature to increase can be determined based on the variations in flow rate used.

Client : PT. Bintang Timur Anugerah (PT. BTA)
Fluidized Bed

The simulation involves combustion in a fluidized bed using a multiphase model. The animation on the side illustrates the results of the simulation in a cross-sectional view, utilizing the OpenFOAM software. The fluidic phenomenon depicts a multiphase flow for compressed gas, employing the RANS (Reynolds Average Navier-Stokes) equations to obtain data in accordance with real-world conditions. As shown in the image, there is a reaction between coal and the fuel gas along with oxygen.

This reaction process involves a fraction of 0.6 and a maximum point of 0.063. Silica particles liquefy with the gas mixture. This reaction takes place in a furnace system that functions to heat water and turn it into steam.

Client : Gadjah Mada University
